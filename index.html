Brand & Legal Guidelines

TRADEMARK &
BRAND USAGE

Trademark symbols show that a name is a Lightmatter trademark and help protect our brand from misuse. The ® symbol marks a registered trademark, and the ™ symbol marks an unregistered one.

They provide notice but are not legally required in every instance. The notice can strengthen our rights if we ever need to enforce a mark, so we mark the first prominent use and let the attribution line cover the rest.

Using the marks

Two rules cover almost everything. Then it’s just where the symbol goes: mark the first or most prominent use, skip it after that, and add the attribution line somewhere in the piece.

Always use a trademark as an adjective that modifies a noun, never as a noun or verb. On first use, pair it with a product or technology name (for example, “Passage® interconnect”); after that, it may be used on its own.

Rule 01

Mark the first instance

Mark the first or most prominent use of a trademark, then drop the symbol on every use after that in the same piece.

Rule 02

Add the attribution line

Include the approved attribution line once per piece, in the footer, closing slide, product docs, or terms of use. It names every Lightmatter trademark in one place.

Logos

Lightmatter logo

  • Keep the LM logo and Lightmatter logo clean everywhere, including the site header, footers, product dashboards, and slide masters.
  • Logos generally do not require a ® or ™ symbol. The attribution line provides trademark notice.
Slides

Presentations

  • Mark each mark’s first use, usually on the title or first content slide. Don’t repeat it after.
  • Mark a standalone slide too, if it might be screenshotted or shared on its own elsewhere.
  • Put the attribution line on a closing legal slide.
Website

Web pages

  • Mark the first use on each page. No symbol after that.
  • Skip nav items and repeated headings; mark the first prominent body or hero use.
  • Keep the attribution line in the global footer.
Product

Engravings & packaging

  • The LM logo and product name don’t need a ® or ™ symbol on the engraving.
  • The attribution line ships with the product instead, on the packaging, datasheet, or an included card, and provides the notice.

Quick reference

Logos No symbol on logos. The attribution line covers them.
Slides Mark the first or most prominent slide per deck. Attribution line on a closing legal slide.
Website Mark the first use per page. Attribution line in the global footer.
Product Optional on the product. The attribution line on packaging, datasheet, or docs provides the notice.
